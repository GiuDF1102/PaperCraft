Starting a Chat with PaperCraft

Engage with PaperCraft's AI-powered assistance by initiating a chat directly within the platform. The chat feature serves as a dynamic channel for users to interact with PaperCraft and seek guidance on refining their academic papers. Whether users require clarification on specific writing conventions, seek recommendations for enhancing clarity, or desire feedback on tone and style, the chat functionality facilitates real-time communication with PaperCraft.

Users are encouraged to provide context regarding the current part of their paper they are working on to ensure that PaperCraft comprehends their requirements accurately. This contextual information enables PaperCraft to offer tailored suggestions and insights aligned with the user's immediate needs. Whether users are crafting an introduction, outlining methodology, presenting results, or concluding their paper, providing context allows PaperCraft to deliver targeted assistance effectively.

In addition to initiating chats, users can also leverage PaperCraft's document upload feature for a comprehensive analysis and enhancement of their text. By hovering over the "New" option in the sidebar, users can access the document upload functionality, allowing them to submit their papers for thorough evaluation and refinement. This feature empowers users to harness PaperCraft's AI capabilities for enhancing the overall quality and coherence of their academic documents.

To open a new chat, users can simply hover over the "New" option and select the new chat button, initiating a fresh conversation with PaperCraft. Whether users prefer real-time interaction via chat or comprehensive document analysis, PaperCraft provides the tools and capabilities necessary to elevate the standard of academic writing and facilitate scholarly communication.

Statistics

These statistics provide an overview of key aspects in evaluating the quality of academic writing. Together, they help ensure that academic text meets standards of professionalism, precision, and accessibility for its intended audience.
  • Tone: Assesses the formality and appropriateness of the writing style for academic communication. A High score reflects a formal and appropriate style for academia. A Low score suggests a tone unsuitable for academic discourse.
  • Grammar: Evaluates the grammatical accuracy and precision in the text. A High score indicates a high level of accuracy and precision. A Low score implies notable errors or imprecision.
  • Clarity: Measures the ease of language and structure. A High score suggests clear and accessible language and structure. A Low score indicates a difficulty in understanding due to complex language or structure.

Settings

These settings serve as guidelines for adjusting the output of generated text based on specific criteria. They allow for customization of various aspects such as style, length, and creativity, ensuring that the generated text aligns with the desired tone, depth, and engagement level. By providing these parameters, users can tailor the text to suit different contexts, purposes, and audiences effectively.
  • Style: The coherence and consistency of the language and writing characteristics within the text
    • Accessible: An "Accessible" writing style refers to text that is easy to understand and comprehend by a wide range of audiences. It avoids complex language, jargon, and technical terms, aiming for clarity and simplicity. This style is often used in educational materials, manuals, and online content where the goal is to ensure that information is easily accessible to all readers regardless of their background or expertise.
    • Standard: A "Standard" writing style adheres to widely accepted norms and conventions of language usage and grammar. It maintains a neutral tone and follows established rules of communication. This style is commonly used in everyday communication, business correspondence, and academic writing where clarity and professionalism are paramount. It aims to convey information accurately and effectively without using overly formal or informal language.
    • Formal: A "Formal" writing style is characterized by its adherence to strict rules of grammar, syntax, and vocabulary. It is often used in academic papers, legal documents, and professional reports where precision and accuracy are essential. Formal writing typically avoids contractions, slang, and colloquialisms, maintaining a serious and authoritative tone. It conveys professionalism and expertise, making it suitable for situations where a high level of formality is expected.
  • Length: The maximum allowed length of the AI’s generated modified text.
    • Short: Provides concise responses or modifications with a focus on brevity and efficiency.
    • Medium: Offers moderately detailed responses or modifications, striking a balance between brevity and thoroughness.
    • Long: Gives comprehensive responses or modifications, offering detailed explanations or suggestions to improve the text.
  • Creativity: Tendency of the AI’s generated text to adhere to predefined patterns or be novel and engaging.
    • Conventional: Follows traditional patterns and structures commonly found in academic or formal writing. It tends to adhere closely to established norms without introducing significant deviations.
    • Neutral: Adapts to the context of the text without introducing significant deviations from conventional patterns. It strikes a balance between adhering to established norms and allowing for some flexibility in expression.
    • Innovative: Introduces novel and engaging elements to the text, potentially deviating from traditional patterns for a more creative output. It seeks to break away from conventional structures and styles to offer fresh and original content.

Sidebar

Chat Section

This section contains the chronology of the chats created by the user, allowing them to navigate through the open chats when needed. Users can easily access their chat history and review past conversations. The chronology of chats is organized based on the time of creation, displaying the most recent chats at the top. Users can click on a chat to open it and continue the conversation, or to reference previous messages and discussions.

Documents Section

This section contains the document history, allowing users to navigate through their uploaded and accessed documents. Users can easily access a chronological list of documents they have uploaded or interacted with in the platform. The document history is organized based on the time of upload or access, displaying the most recent documents at the top. Users can click on a document to open it and view its contents and continue reviewing it, or to make edits.

Sections of Open Documents

This section displays the sections of each document (paper) uploaded by the user, enabling easy navigation through the content of each document. When a document is selected, its sections are listed hierarchically, allowing users to quickly locate specific parts of the document. For example, in a research paper, sections may include abstract, introduction, methodology, results, discussion, and conclusion. Users can click on a section to navigate directly to that part of the document, facilitating efficient review and editing.
